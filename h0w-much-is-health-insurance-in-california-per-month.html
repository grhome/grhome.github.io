How Much is Health Insurance in California Per Month?

Buy A Plan

Health insurance is one of those necessary evils that can be quite the financial puzzle. Whether you're a resident or considering moving to the Golden State, knowing what to expect in terms of monthly premiums can help you plan better. Let's explore this step by step.


Health insurance costs can vary widely based on several factors including age, location, income, and the type of plan you choose. California offers a range of options through its state exchange, Covered California, as well as through private insurers. Generally, monthly premiums can range from less than $100 to over $1,000, but this depends greatly on the specifics of the plan and the individual's circumstances.


Factors Influencing Health Insurance Costs

1. Age:

Younger Adults: If you're in your twenties or early thirties, you're in luck. Younger individuals typically enjoy lower premiums since they are considered lower risk.
Older Adults: As you age, premiums tend to increase. This is because older adults generally require more medical care.


2. Income:

Subsidies and Assistance: In California, many people qualify for subsidies under the Affordable Care Act (ACA). These subsidies can significantly lower your monthly premium if your income falls within certain limits. For example, a single individual earning up to around $54,000 annually might qualify for some form of subsidy.

Without Subsidies: If your income is higher, you'll be paying the full premium, which can be quite substantial.


3. Location:

Urban vs. Rural: Where you live in California can impact your premium. Urban areas like Los Angeles or San Francisco might have different rates compared to rural areas due to the cost of living and the availability of healthcare providers.


4. Plan Type:

HMO vs. PPO: Health Maintenance Organization (HMO) plans generally have lower premiums but require you to use a network of doctors and hospitals. Preferred Provider Organization (PPO) plans offer more flexibility in choosing healthcare providers but come with higher premiums.


Metal Tiers: Plans are categorized into Bronze, Silver, Gold, and Platinum tiers. Bronze plans have the lowest premiums but highest out-of-pocket costs, while Platinum plans have the highest premiums but lowest out-of-pocket costs.


Average Costs for 2024

To give you a clearer picture, let's look at some average costs for different scenarios in 2024:


For a Single Adult

  • Bronze Plan: Around $400 - $500 per month
  • Silver Plan: Approximately $500 - $700 per month
  • Gold Plan: Between $600 - $800 per month
  • Platinum Plan: Roughly $800 - $1,000+ per month

For a Family of Four

  • Bronze Plan: Around $1,200 - $1,500 per month
  • Silver Plan: Approximately $1,500 - $2,000 per month
  • Gold Plan: Between $1,800 - $2,500 per month
  • Platinum Plan: Roughly $2,500 - $3,200+ per month

These numbers can vary based on the specifics mentioned earlier, but they provide a ballpark figure to work with.


Covered California:

This is California's health insurance marketplace where you can compare and purchase health plans. Covered California also helps determine if you qualify for subsidies. Open enrollment typically begins in November and runs through January, but special enrollment periods are available if you experience a qualifying life event like losing other coverage, getting married, or having a baby.



Medi-Cal:

California’s Medicaid program, Medi-Cal, provides free or low-cost health coverage to eligible individuals and families with low incomes. The income thresholds for Medi-Cal are higher than in many other states, making it a viable option for more people.

Saving on Health Insurance

  • Shop Around:

    • Use resources like Covered California to compare different plans and see what fits your needs and budget. Don’t just settle for the first plan you come across.


  • Consider High-Deductible Plans:

    • If you’re generally healthy and don’t expect many medical expenses, a high-deductible health plan (HDHP) with lower premiums might be a good option. Just be sure you can cover the higher out-of-pocket costs if necessary.


  • Take Advantage of Subsidies:

    • Make sure to check if you qualify for any subsidies or tax credits. These can make a significant difference in your monthly premium.


  • Healthy Lifestyle Discounts:

    • Some insurers offer discounts if you maintain a healthy lifestyle. Participating in wellness programs or even just non-smoking can sometimes lower your premiums.
