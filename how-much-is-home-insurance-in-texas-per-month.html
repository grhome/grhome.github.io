How Much is Home Insurance in Texas Per Month?

You’re standing on the sun-kissed plains of Texas, gazing at the sprawling landscapes, cowboy hats, and the promise of adventure. But wait—what about protecting your cozy abode from life’s unexpected twists?
In this article, we embark on a journey to demystify the enigma of home insurance costs in the great state of Texas. Buckle up, fellow Texans (and curious readers), as we unravel the secrets behind those monthly premiums!


Average Home Insurance Cost

Let’s cut to the chase. The average cost of home insurance in Texas dances around $4,400 per year, which translates to roughly $367 per month.

Before you gasp or reach for your calculator, let’s put this in perspective. Nationally, the average annual home insurance rate is a more modest $1,915 per year. So, why the Texan twist?


Factors Affecting the Home Insurance Rates

  • Location: Texas isn’t just big; it’s colossal! Your precise spot on this vast canvas matters. Coastal areas, with their salty breezes and hurricane whispers, might nudge those premiums upward.

  • Dwelling Coverage: How much is your humble abode worth? The value of your home (aka dwelling coverage) plays a starring role. Bigger homes, fancier finishes—expect a grander bill.

  • Liability Limelight: Liability coverage steps onto the stage. It’s like having a protective forcefield around your property. The more coverage you choose, the more it waltzes with your premium.

  • Deductible Drama: Imagine a Wild West showdown: you versus your deductible. A higher deductible means lower monthly payments. Choose wisely, partner

Best Affordable Options

Now, let’s meet our trusty steeds—the home insurance companies that ride the Texan range:

  • State Farm: Yeehaw! State Farm gallops in with an average homeowners insurance rate of $4,020 per year. That’s below the state average. Their secret sauce? Extra dwelling coverage (just in case your home needs a makeover after a covered disaster). And hold your horses—they throw in a free Ting device. What’s that, you ask? It’s a smart plug that keeps an eye on your home’s electrical network, preventing fiery surprises.

  • Chubb: If you’re allergic to consumer complaints, Chubb’s your amigo. They prioritize customer satisfaction like a well-seasoned chili recipe. Comprehensive coverage? Check. Happy customers? Double-check.

Note: USAA offers an even cheaper rate in Texas. But here’s the twist: it’s exclusively for active military, veterans, and their families.


As the sun sets over the Texas horizon, remember this: home insurance isn’t just about numbers—it’s about peace of mind. So, whether you’re a rancher, a city slicker, or a two-steppin’ enthusiast, find the right policy. Protect your homestead, and may your premiums ride off into the sunset!

How can I lower my home insurance costs in Texas?

There are several ways to lower your home insurance costs in Texas:

  1. Ask for Discounts: Insurance companies often offer various discounts. For example, you might qualify for a safe home discount if you have a security alarm, or a smoke alarm or sprinkler discount.

  2. Bundling Policies: You could get a discount for having two or more policies (like home and car) with the same company.

  3. Make Your Home More Disaster-Resistant: Upgrading your home to withstand severe weather can lead to lower premiums.

  4. New Home or Renovation Discount: If your home was built or renovated in the last five years, your insurance company might reduce your premium.

  5. Roof Discount: Companies often offer discounts for roofs made of materials that help prevent damage or resist fire. Homes with newer roofs may get a roof age discount.

Remember, it’s always a good idea to shop around and compare rates from different insurance companies to ensure you’re getting the best deal. You can also consult with insurance agents or brokers for personalized advice.
