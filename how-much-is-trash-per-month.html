How Much Is Trash Per Month?

Know your Bill

Have you ever stopped to think about the journey your trash takes after you toss it into the bin? It’s a fascinating process, and one that comes with a cost. In this article, we’ll explore the average cost of trash per month, the factors that influence these costs, and how you can potentially reduce your monthly garbage bill.


How Much Is Trash Per Month?

The cost of trash removal varies greatly depending on where you live, the size of your containers, and the local competition. On average, most households in the United States pay anywhere from $20 to $80 per month for trash and garbage collection services. However, average cost of trash removal per month can fluctuate widely by region.


What Factors Influence the Cost of Trash Removal?

Several factors can influence the cost of your monthly garbage bill. Let’s take a closer look at some of them:

  • Location

    • Your geographical location plays a significant role in determining your trash removal costs. For instance, if you live in a city or larger town, your community might provide trash collection services1. In other areas, you might have to rely on providers like Waste Management or Republic Services.


  • Size of Your Containers

    • The size of your garbage containers can also impact your monthly bill. For example, in Castro Valley, California, the monthly rates for garbage carts range from $23.17 for a 20-gallon cart to $88.91 for a 96-gallon cart.


  • Type of Waste

    • Not all refuse is actually trash. The contents of your kitchen garbage bag aren’t classified the same way as that old sofa you want out of your basement. How your trash is classified determines when it is picked up and whether there is an additional charge for picking it up.


How Can You Reduce Your Monthly Garbage Bill?

There are several strategies you can employ to reduce your monthly garbage bill. One of the most effective ways is to reduce the amount of waste you produce. The average American produces 4.4 pounds of trash every day. By reducing, reusing, and recycling, you can significantly cut down on the amount of trash you generate, potentially lowering your monthly garbage bill.


In conclusion, the cost of trash per month can vary widely depending on a variety of factors. By understanding these costs and taking steps to reduce your waste, you can potentially save money on your monthly garbage bill. Remember, every little bit helps – not just for your wallet, but for the environment too!
