How Much is Obamacare a Month for a Single Person?

Add a Plan

Obamacare, officially known as the Affordable Care Act (ACA), has been a significant milestone in the American healthcare system. It has made health insurance accessible to millions of Americans who were previously uninsured. However, the cost of Obamacare varies significantly based on several factors such as the plan tier, the insurance company, the age of the individual, and the network type of the plan. Let’s delve into these factors to understand the monthly cost of Obamacare for a single person.


Monthly Obamacare Cost by Plan Tier

Obamacare plans are categorized into four metal tiers: Bronze, Silver, Gold, and Platinum. Each tier represents a different level of cost-sharing between the insurer and the insured.

  1. Bronze: These plans have the lowest monthly premiums but the highest out-of-pocket costs when accessing care. On average, the plan pays 60% of healthcare expenses, while the individual pays 40%.

  2. Silver: Silver plans have moderately priced monthly premiums. They are a good choice for most people as they balance the monthly premium with the out-of-pocket costs.

  3. Gold: Gold plans have higher premiums but lower out-of-pocket costs. They are suitable for individuals who require frequent medical care.

  4. Platinum: These plans have the highest monthly premiums but the lowest out-of-pocket costs. They are ideal for individuals with high medical costs.

The average monthly costs for a 40-year-old are $462 for Bronze, $584 for Silver, and $641 for Gold.


Monthly Obamacare Cost by Company

The cost of Obamacare also varies by the insurance company. Major health insurance companies in the ACA marketplace include Aetna, Blue Cross Blue Shield, and Cigna. Each company offers plans in all 50 states and Washington, D.C., with a vast network of providers. The average monthly cost for a 40-year-old individual is $4691.


Monthly Obamacare Cost by Age

Age is a significant factor in determining the cost of Obamacare. For instance, a 30-year-old pays $412 monthly on average for an unsubsidized ACA plan. This cost increases to $469 for a 40-year-old, $655 for a 50-year-old, and $994 for a 60-year-old.


Monthly Obamacare Cost by Network Type

The type of network plan also influences the cost of Obamacare. Health Maintenance Organization (HMO) and Exclusive Provider Organization (EPO) plans typically have lower premiums but require members to use a specific network of providers. On the other hand, Preferred Provider Organization (PPO) and Point of Service (POS) plans offer more flexibility in choosing providers but come with higher premiums.


In conclusion, the monthly cost of Obamacare for a single person depends on various factors. It’s essential to consider these factors and your healthcare needs when choosing a plan. Remember, while the cost is a crucial aspect, it should not be the only factor in your decision-making process. Always consider the coverage, out-of-pocket costs, and the network of providers when selecting a health insurance plan.
